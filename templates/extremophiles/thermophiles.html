Heat Adaptation

Thermophiles

Organisms adapted to high temperatures, from geothermal pools to deep-sea vents.

Pyrococcus furiosus: The angry ball of fire that pioneered the field of thermophile research.

Pyrococcus furiosus was first discovered in 1986 by Stetter and Co when archaea were still referred to as archaebacteria. It was described as "furious" for its fast growth rate and aggressive motility. Unlike the typical thermophile, these archaea are capable of utilizing starches to grow and are strict anaerobic heterotrophs. Pyrococcus furiosus continued to break trends with its strange glycolytic pathway. Most notable, however, is the archaea's unique DNA polymerase and its applications in PCR reactions, having a higher fidelity than the other common polymerase, Taq.

Geobacillus stearothermophilus: The heat-proof benchmark for sterilization.

Sulfolobus solfataricus: The archaea that shrugs off heat and radiation.
